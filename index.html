Rural & extended pickup

On-demand rides work well close to town, but they thin out fast in rural areas. For members further out, your Navigator can arrange extended pickup through local volunteer-driver networks or non-emergency medical transport partners, for an added assist fee. You'll always know the cost before you book — no surprises.

Why vetting matters

Senior-focused ride services already exist, and their track record is instructive: riders have reported cancellations, per-minute fees they weren't told about upfront, and vehicles that couldn't accommodate a walker or wheelchair. That's exactly the standard Saunterby vets against — every driver network we work with is checked for licensing, insurance, and accessibility capability before we use it, and every fee is disclosed before a member books, not after.

PACE participants

PACE (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly) works differently — participants receive transportation and recreational services through their own PACE care team, and generally can't book outside services directly. For PACE members, Saunterby's role is to become a vetted community activity partner that a participant's care team can choose to arrange, rather than something booked directly through Saunterby.

Choosing your plan

Two ways to belong.
Both make sense.

Set Plan

A steady, known amount each month

Built for members working with a fixed monthly amount — a benefit-card allowance, a set budget, or simply a number you'd rather not think about twice. Your Navigator helps you get the most from it, and you'll never be surprised by the total.

Good fit if: you're spending benefit dollars, or you like knowing the number in advance.
Open Plan

Spend as much as feels right

Built for members who'd rather not cap a good thing. Book as often as you like, add experiences as they catch your eye, and settle up on your own schedule.

Good fit if: your calendar is the priority and the budget is flexible.
